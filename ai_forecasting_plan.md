# AI-Based Feed Consumption Forecasting

> **Phase:** System Design → AI Implementation Plan (Layer Egg Focus)  
> **Status:** Draft  
> **Version:** 2.0 (Revised)

This document describes the approach to building, training, and deploying the AI model to predict feed consumption for **Laying Hens**. Accurate forecasting is critical in layer farms because underfeeding causes severe drops in egg production that take weeks to recover, while overfeeding wastes money and can cause fatty liver syndrome in hens.

---

## 1. Problem Definition

*   **Goal:** Predict the daily feed requirement (in kg) for a specific laying flock over the upcoming 1 to 7 days.
*   **Type of AI Problem:** Supervised Time-Series Regression.
*   **Target Variable (Y):** `feed_consumed_kg` (for Day *t+1*, *t+2*, etc.).

---

## 2. Recommended AI Models (For MVP)

For an initial version, tree-based machine learning models or sliding-window regression are optimal.

1.  **XGBoost / LightGBM:** 
    *   *Why:* Highly accurate with tabular farm data. Excellent at capturing the non-linear relationship between the flock's egg-laying curve (which peaks around week 25 and slowly declines) and its energy (feed) requirement.
2.  **Random Forest Regressor:**
    *   *Why:* Robust and less prone to overfitting on smaller, early-stage farm datasets.

---

## 3. Feature Engineering (The Inputs - 'X')

Unlike broilers whose feed intake skyrockets daily as they grow, adult laying hens have stable body weights. Their feed intake is primarily driven by their **egg production output**, **age**, and **environmental temperature**.

**Core Input Features (From Database):**
*   `age_in_weeks` - Extracted from `flocks.placement_date` and `age_at_placement_wks`.
*   `current_live_birds_count` - How many hens are actively eating.
*   `hen_day_production_pct` - The current Laying Rate (%) derived from `total_sellable_eggs / current_live_birds`. (Higher production = higher feed requirement).
*   `temperature_c` - High temperatures reduce feed intake as hens pant and drink more water instead.

**Time-Series Lag Features (Generated by Backend):**
*   `feed_consumed_lag_1` - Feed consumed yesterday.
*   `feed_consumed_lag_7` - Feed consumed one week ago.
*   `production_pct_rolling_avg` - The 7-day moving average of the Laying Rate (smooths out daily collection anomalies).

---

## 4. Backend Integration (FastAPI Ecosystem)

### A. The Training Pipeline (Weekly Job)
1.  A CRON job runs `train_layer_model.py`.
2.  Queries historical `daily_flock_records` and `daily_egg_production` tables across all active and depleted flocks.
3.  Calculates the running `hen_day_production_pct` and merges it with feed data.
4.  Trains the **XGBoost** model.
5.  Saves the trained model (e.g., `model_layer_v1.pkl`).

### B. The Inference Pipeline (On-Demand Prediction)
1.  Manager clicks "Forecast Feed Needs" on the AI Dashboard.
2.  API endpoint `POST /api/forecasts/predict/{flock_id}` is triggered.
3.  FastAPI leverages `model_layer_v1.pkl`.
4.  FastAPI fetches the flock's current week-age, live count, and last 7 days of egg production data.
5.  The model outputs future feed values (e.g., `[115.0, 115.5, 116.0, 118.0, 115.2]`).
6.  The backend saves these to `ai_forecast_results` and returns JSON to the UI.

---

## 5. Refining & Feedback Loop

The AI gets smarter over time. Because the `ai_forecast_results` table stores the `predicted_feed_kg` side-by-side with a blank `actual_feed_kg`, the system corrects itself continuously.

*   When a farm worker logs tomorrow’s actual feed consumption in `daily_flock_records`, a background task updates the `actual_feed_kg` column in the forecast table.
*   The dashboard calculates the **MAPE** (Mean Absolute Percentage Error) to show the manager the model's accuracy. Over time, as seasons change (Summer vs Winter), the model will learn how temperature swings impact the layer flock's appetite.

The full System Design (Database, API, Frontend, and AI) has now been fully pivoted and tailored for a **Layer Egg Farm** focusing strongly on egg inventory, grading, and laying-rate driven feed predictions.
